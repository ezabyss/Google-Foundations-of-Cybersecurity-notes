## ‚ö†Ô∏è Threats

A **threat** is any event or circumstance that can negatively impact an asset.

### Social Engineering

* A manipulation technique that exploits **human error**
* Used to gain private information, access, or valuables

#### Phishing (Common Social Engineering Attack)

* Uses emails or messages that appear legitimate
* Contains malicious links or attachments
* Aims to steal:

  * Usernames
  * Passwords
  * Banking information

## üìâ Risks

A **risk** is the likelihood that a threat will affect an asset by impacting:

* **Confidentiality**
* **Integrity**
* **Availability**

### Example of Risk

* No backup protocols in place
* Data cannot be recovered after an accident or security incident

### Risk Levels

Organizations classify assets based on risk level:

#### üü¢ Low Risk

* Publicly available information
* Minimal impact if compromised
* Examples:

  * Website content
  * Published research data

#### üü° Medium Risk

* Not public information
* Moderate financial or reputational impact
* Example:

  * Early release of quarterly earnings affecting stock prices

#### üî¥ High Risk

* Protected by laws and regulations
* Severe impact if compromised
* Examples:

  * PII or SPII leaks
  * Intellectual property theft

## üß± Vulnerabilities

A **vulnerability** is a weakness that can be exploited by a threat.

> A **risk exists only when both a threat and a vulnerability are present**.

### Common Vulnerabilities

* Outdated firewalls, software, or applications
* Weak passwords
* Unprotected confidential data

### People as Vulnerabilities

Human actions can significantly affect security:

* Employees
* Clients
* External vendors

Security is a **shared responsibility** across the organization.

## üë• Analyst Responsibilities in Reducing Risk

Entry-level analysts help reduce vulnerabilities by:

* Encouraging reporting of suspicious activity

* Supporting physical security controls

### Examples of Good Practices

* Security awareness training
* Access cards for physical entry

## ü¶† Ransomware

**Ransomware** is a costly type of malware.

### How Ransomware Works

* Threat actors encrypt organizational data
* Systems and devices become unusable
* A ransom is demanded in exchange for a **decryption key**

> **Decryption key**: A password used to restore access to encrypted data.

Ransom negotiations and data leaks often occur through the **dark web**.
